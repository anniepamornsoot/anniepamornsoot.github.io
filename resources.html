Quick answers

Questions Annie gets
asked every week.

Is Phuket safe for families with young children?

+

Yes — Phuket is generally very safe for families. Road safety is the most relevant concern. Most expat families with young kids drive cars rather than scooters. Neighbourhoods like Bangtao and Cherngtalay are quiet and low-risk day-to-day.

Do I need to speak Thai to live comfortably in Phuket?

+

No. English is widely spoken in the areas where most expats live — in shops, restaurants, hospitals, and most services you'll use day-to-day.

How far in advance should I start planning the move?

+

For families with school-age children, 6–12 months is ideal — school enrollment and waiting lists require lead time. For couples without children or retirees, 3–6 months is usually enough.

Should I rent first before deciding where to buy?

+

Almost always, yes. Renting for 6–12 months lets you experience an area through different seasons, understand your actual routine, and make a much more informed purchase decision.

What's the best time of year to visit before relocating?

+

November to April is peak season — dry, sunny, and the easiest time to experience Phuket at its best. If you want to test how you handle the low season, come in August or September during the monsoon.
