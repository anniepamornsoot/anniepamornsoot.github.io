The story

Why I built Phuket Living.

I moved to Phuket in 2017 with my partner, two suitcases, and a lot of questions nobody could answer properly. We ended up in the wrong area for six months — beautiful property, wrong lifestyle fit.

When we eventually found the right neighbourhood, everything shifted. The kids settled. Our routine clicked. We stopped feeling like we were on a long holiday and started feeling like we actually lived here.

"I realised the property was never really the decision. The area was the decision — and nobody had helped us make it properly."

That experience became the foundation of Phuket Living. Every client I work with starts with the same question: how do you actually want to live? The property comes second. The life comes first.

What I believe

Five things that guide
every conversation.

1

Choosing the right area matters more than choosing the right property

A great home in the wrong neighbourhood will never feel right. We start with lifestyle, not listings.

2

You don't need more options — you need the right ones

I send clients three or four carefully matched properties, not forty. Decision fatigue is real.

3

Clarity removes fear

Clear, honest information — even when it's not what you hoped to hear — is always more useful than reassurance.

4

There is no "best" property — only what's right for your life

I'm not here to sell the most expensive property. I'm here to find the most fitting one.

5

Relocating is a life decision, not a property transaction

You're choosing a school run, a weekend pace, a community. I treat the decision with the weight it deserves.

How I'm different

What Phuket Living is not.

Not a listing aggregator. I don't publish everything available. I publish what I'd actually recommend — and only after understanding your situation.

Not commission-driven in the traditional sense. My incentive is a client who settles well and refers others — not a fast close on the highest-value property.

Not a remote service. I accompany every viewing in person. There are no virtual tours dressed up as local expertise.

Not one-size-fits-all. A retired couple from Germany and a remote-working family from Sydney have almost nothing in common when it comes to the right area.
