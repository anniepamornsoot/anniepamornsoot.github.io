Choosing a school in Phuket:
what no one tells you on the tour.

By Annie · Phuket Living · March 2026 · 12 min read

Every week, a parent sends me the same message: we've got three school tours booked, what do we ask? After seven years on the island and dozens of visits, here are the four questions I wish someone had handed me before my first open day.

International schools in Phuket are a different animal from the ones you're used to back home. The glossy brochures, the perfectly-framed classroom photos, the admissions officers in matching polos — they're all polished. And they should be: this is a decision that shapes the next ten years of your child's life and a significant portion of your family's budget.

But polished doesn't mean transparent. And after dozens of tours, I've learned that the questions that actually matter aren't the ones printed on the prospectus.

1. What's the staff turnover rate?

The most important question almost nobody asks. High teacher turnover is the single strongest predictor of how your child's year will actually feel — more than curriculum, more than facilities, more than class size.

A school with 25% annual turnover means every fourth teacher your child meets will be gone next September. A school with 8% turnover means consistency, continuity, and a culture worth staying for.

Ask: "What's your staff turnover rate in the last three years, and what's driving it?" Watch how quickly they answer.

“ Ask the question you hope they'll flinch at. The flinch tells you more than the brochure ever will.

2. Who's leaving this year — and why?

Every family that leaves is a data point. Some leave because they're repatriating. Some because the dad's contract ended. But some leave because the school wasn't the right fit — and those are the stories the admissions office will never volunteer.

The honest answer to this question tells you whether the school takes its leavers seriously or treats them as inevitable churn.

3. What happens when my child is struggling?

Every parent wants to know what the top student does. Almost no one asks what the middle-of-the-road student gets. And yet most of our children will be middle-of-the-road students at some point — in maths, in friendships, in the first term of a new country.

Ask what the support system looks like for the kid who's having a bad month. Ask about counsellors, learning support staff, and how teachers communicate with parents. The answer tells you whether pastoral care is a system or a slogan.

4. Can I speak to three current parents?

A confident school will say yes within 24 hours. A school that's unwilling or slow to connect you is hiding something — or doesn't trust its own parent community enough to let them speak candidly.

When you do speak to those parents, ask them: what would you change if you could? Every parent has something. The question is whether the school is working on it.

A word on fees

The headline figure is rarely the real figure. Once you add capital fees, enrollment fees, bus service, uniforms, after-school clubs, trips, and lunch, the published tuition can easily double.

Ask for an itemised "all-in" figure for the first year. Then compare schools on that number, not the headline one.

A final thought

The right school for your child isn't always the most famous, the newest, or the closest. It's the one where, on a tour, you feel your shoulders drop. Where the kids in the corridor look happy, not performative. Where the teachers smile at each other, not just at you.

Trust that feeling. And if you want a second set of eyes, I'm always just a message away.
