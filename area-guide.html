Area guide · South Phuket

Rawai — quiet, local,
genuinely Phuket.

If Bangtao feels like an expat suburb, Rawai feels like a town. It has a slower rhythm, a stronger local identity, and sea views that most areas can't match.

Annie's honest take

"Rawai is for people who value peace over proximity. If schools aren't urgent, it's often the most liveable area in Phuket."

Daily life

What living here
actually feels like.

Morning routine

Walk or drive to Rawai seafront for coffee and a local breakfast. Grab fresh fish from the pier market. Very few tourists this early — it's genuinely local.

Schooling

No international schools in Rawai itself — the closest are about 15–20 minutes away. Manageable with a car, but worth knowing before committing.

Getting around

You'll need a car or scooter. Rawai isn't walkable to supermarkets, but Nai Harn beach and Chalong pier are within 10 minutes.

Healthcare

Chalong Hospital is 10 minutes away. Bangkok Hospital Phuket is around 25 minutes. Adequate for day-to-day, with solid options for anything more serious.

Weekends

Nai Harn beach, Windmill viewpoint, Promthep Cape, diving trips from Chalong pier. Some of the best sunsets in Phuket — without the crowds.

The honest picture

What Rawai gets
right — and what it doesn't.

What works well

Some of the best sea views and sunsets in Phuket

Noticeably quieter than the north — you feel the difference immediately

Strong local market scene and authentic Thai restaurants

Lower rents than Bangtao for equivalent space

Growing expat community with a more grounded, less resort-y feel

What to consider

No international schools in the area — a 15–20 min daily drive is required

Not walkable — a car or scooter is essential, not optional

Fewer Western restaurants and supermarkets than Bangtao

Further from the airport (~45 min) — relevant if you travel frequently
